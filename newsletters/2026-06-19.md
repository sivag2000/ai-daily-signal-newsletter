# The Agentic Era Accelerates
**Issue #024 · June 19, 2026**
*Agents, acquisitions, and infrastructure reshape the AI landscape.*

## TL;DR
- **Google** unveils "agentic" Gemini features, enabling proactive, 24/7 AI assistance for businesses and individuals.
- **OpenAI** launches a $150 million Partner Network and acquires Ona, enhancing enterprise AI deployment and agent capabilities.
- **Amazon Web Services** will sell its custom AI chips to other data centers, directly challenging Nvidia in the hardware market.

## THE BIG STORY
The promise of AI moving beyond simple chatbots into proactive, intelligent assistants is rapidly materializing, marking the dawn of what Google terms the "agentic Gemini era." At its I/O 2026 conference, Google showcased Gemini’s enhanced capabilities to deliver truly agentic, 24/7 help—from autonomously organizing a semester’s worth of notes into study guides to providing complex disease management support, as detailed in new research on its medical AI, **AMIE**. This shift positions AI to anticipate needs, manage workflows, and operate with greater autonomy across various applications.

This progression towards agentic AI is not confined to Google. OpenAI is aggressively expanding its ecosystem to support enterprise adoption of advanced AI, including long-running agents. The company announced the **OpenAI Partner Network**, investing **$150 million** to accelerate AI transformation globally, alongside its planned acquisition of **Ona**. This acquisition aims to expand **Codex** with secure, persistent cloud environments, critical for developing sophisticated AI agents that can operate across complex enterprise workflows without constant human oversight.

The implications are profound for businesses and developers alike. AI is no longer just a tool for generating text or images; it's evolving into a foundational layer that can orchestrate tasks, solve problems proactively, and integrate deeply into operations. This means a new frontier for productivity, but also demands a re-evaluation of security, cost management, and the ethical guardrails required for increasingly autonomous systems. Companies that adapt quickly to this agentic paradigm will gain significant competitive advantages, while others risk falling behind.

## MORE SIGNALS
### AWS Challenges Nvidia with Custom AI Chips
Amazon Web Services (AWS) is reportedly in talks to sell its custom AI chips to other data centers, directly challenging Nvidia's dominance in the AI hardware market. CEO Andy Jassy sees this as a **$50 billion** opportunity for Amazon. This strategic move aims to diversify the supply chain for AI infrastructure and reduce reliance on a single vendor, potentially lowering costs and increasing innovation across the industry.

### AI Investment Remains Robust, Even for Niche Solutions
The "inference gold rush" continues as AI startups secure significant funding. **Baseten**, an AI inference startup, is reportedly close to finalizing a **$1.5 billion** round at a **$13 billion** valuation just months after its last mega-round. Additionally, **Elastic** has agreed to acquire **DeductiveAI**, a three-year-old startup using AI to catch and resolve software bugs, for **up to $85 million**. These investments highlight sustained confidence in specialized AI applications and the infrastructure powering them.

### Regulatory Friction and AI Safety in the Spotlight
The White House is actively navigating the complexities of AI regulation, particularly concerning safety and export controls. Officials are demanding that **Anthropic** block all "jailbreaks" on its **Fable 5** model before it can be re-released, though security experts suggest this may not be fully possible. This situation underscores the ongoing tension between rapid AI development and the imperative to ensure models cannot be misused—a critical area as governments try to establish rules in real time.

## WHAT TO WATCH
The accelerating pace of agentic AI development, exemplified by Google and OpenAI, will continue to reshape how businesses and individuals interact with technology. Expect further innovation in AI's ability to perform complex, multi-step tasks autonomously. Concurrently, the competition in AI hardware—with Amazon challenging Nvidia—and the evolving landscape of AI regulation, especially around model safety and export controls, will be critical trends to monitor.
