# AI's Unstoppable Advance
**Issue #025 · June 20, 2026**
*Navigating rapid AI innovation amidst geopolitical currents and market shifts.*

## TL;DR
- US export controls on Anthropic's **Fable 5** proved largely ineffective, with market growth continuing despite the ban.
- OpenAI gears up for its IPO, recruiting top talent, launching new enterprise features, and a **$150M** partner network.
- Google advances its "agentic Gemini era" with new business tools and significant research breakthroughs in health AI.

## THE BIG STORY
The US government’s attempt to halt Anthropic’s release of its **Fable 5** and **Mythos** AI models over national security concerns has illuminated a critical tension between regulation and technological momentum. Despite the ban, which aimed to prevent the distribution of advanced cybersecurity models, market indicators suggest the move has done little to slow Anthropic's overall trajectory or impact its anticipated IPO. History shows that similar efforts to control cybersecurity-related software exports have been ineffective over the last **30 years**, raising questions about the feasibility of containing leading-edge AI.

This situation puts developers relying on Anthropic's platform in a challenging position, while also inadvertently boosting the company's profile by drawing significant media attention. Regulatory officials from the White House have reportedly demanded Anthropic block all "jailbreaks"—a standard security challenge for large language models that experts widely consider an impossible guarantee. The incident underscores the nascent and often reactive nature of AI governance, with rules being formulated in real time, often struggling to keep pace with rapid innovation and the global reach of AI development.

The broader implication is a growing disconnect between regulatory intent and market reality. As the "inference gold rush" continues, companies like Anthropic face a complex landscape where national security concerns clash with open development and market demand. This incident highlights the need for more adaptable and globally coordinated approaches to AI regulation, especially as advanced models become increasingly pivotal across industries.

## MORE SIGNALS
### OpenAI’s Strategic Expansion Ahead of IPO
OpenAI is aggressively expanding its capabilities and market reach in preparation for its anticipated IPO. The company recently secured **Transformer** co-inventor **Noam Shazeer** from Google DeepMind and former Trump AI policy official **Dean Ball**, signaling a dual focus on technological leadership and policy engagement. Concurrently, OpenAI launched a **$150 million** Partner Network to accelerate enterprise AI adoption and introduced new usage analytics and spend controls for **ChatGPT Enterprise**, enhancing scalability and cost management for corporate clients. Furthermore, OpenAI announced the acquisition of **Ona** to expand **Codex** with secure, persistent cloud environments, supporting long-running AI agents across workflows.

### Google's "Agentic Gemini Era" Accelerates
Google is deepening its commitment to agentic AI, showcasing significant advancements at I/O 2026. The **Gemini** app is evolving to offer proactive, 24/7 assistance, complemented by new tools specifically designed for businesses and entrepreneurs. Beyond consumer and business applications, Google is making strides in scientific and medical AI; its **AMIE** conversational AI system has matched primary care physicians in complex disease management, and researchers are collaborating with **Co-Scientist** to tackle scientific problems. Google also introduced an experiment improving group meetings on **Google Beam**, using immersive displays and spatial audio to enhance social connection and participation for remote attendees.

### Global AI Adoption and Investment Surge
The global push for AI integration and investment continues unabated. In India, billionaire **Mukesh Ambani's Reliance** is weaving AI into telecom services, aiming to reach over **500 million** people and integrate AI into "every call, app, and home." On the funding front, AI inference startup **Baseten** is reportedly finalizing a **$1.5 billion** round at a **$13 billion** valuation, mere months after its last major funding. The enterprise AI landscape also saw activity with **Elastic** agreeing to acquire **Deductive AI**, a software bug-catching AI startup founded just three years ago, for up to **$85 million**.

## WHAT TO WATCH
The dynamic interplay between government regulation and rapid technological advancement will remain a critical theme. Expect continued debate and new policy proposals as authorities grapple with controlling powerful AI models while the industry pushes for broader deployment. The "agentic AI" paradigm, exemplified by Google's Gemini and OpenAI's expanded enterprise offerings, will drive significant shifts in how businesses and individuals interact with AI, potentially accelerating the enterprise AI adoption curve globally.
