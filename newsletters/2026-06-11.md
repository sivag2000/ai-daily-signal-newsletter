# The Public AI Race
**Issue #016 · June 11, 2026**
*Foundational models seek public markets as agentic AI reshapes interaction.*

## TL;DR
- OpenAI and Anthropic confidentially filed for IPOs, signaling a new public market era for AI giants.
- Google I/O 2026 introduced the "agentic Gemini era," enhancing proactive, personalized AI assistance.
- The AI spending surge continues: Amazon secured **$17.5B** for AI, while firms spend **$7,500** monthly per employee.

## THE BIG STORY
The AI industry is entering a new era of public scrutiny and capital demands, as OpenAI confirmed its confidential S-1 submission to the SEC, following closely on the heels of rival Anthropic, which also filed to go public. These moves underscore a pivotal moment for the sector, shifting from private funding frenzies to the rigorous demands of public markets. It signals a maturation of the foundational model space, where companies are now seeking massive infusions of capital to scale infrastructure, accelerate research, and commercialize their increasingly powerful AI systems.

For investors, these IPOs represent an opportunity to directly participate in the growth of leading AI innovators, but also bring heightened scrutiny on valuations, profitability, and long-term ethical governance. Executives and business leaders across industries will be watching closely, as the performance and transparency of these bellwether AI companies will likely influence broader investment trends and regulatory discussions surrounding artificial general intelligence (AGI) and its societal impact. The shift to public ownership will inevitably shape how these companies balance rapid innovation with calls for safety and accountability, affecting everyone building with or relying on their advanced AI capabilities.

## MORE SIGNALS

### Agentic AI Takes Center Stage
Google’s I/O 2026 highlighted its new "agentic Gemini era," focusing on proactive, 24/7 assistance and enhanced productivity tools for businesses. A new experiment bringing better group meetings to Google Beam, their true-to-life video communication platform, renders participants in true size with spatial audio, fostering a **50% stronger sense of social connection** and a **21% increase** in reported ability to contribute. Meanwhile, OpenAI plans to acquire **Ona** to expand its **Codex** offering with secure, persistent cloud environments, enabling long-running AI agents across enterprise workflows.

### AI Safety and Oversight Under Fire
xAI faces a lawsuit alleging a former engineer was fired for raising safety concerns about its **Grok** model days before SpaceX's IPO. Concurrently, Anthropic issued an apology regarding "invisible" distillation guardrails on its **Claude Fable** model, which had frustrated cybersecurity researchers by being overly strict for their work. Anthropic has since clarified its approach, offering **Claude Mythos 5** to trusted organizations for sensitive tasks and a publicly available **Claude Fable 5** version designed to prevent misuse for cyberattacks.

### The Soaring Cost of the AI Race
The intense competition in AI continues to drive massive capital expenditure. Amazon recently borrowed **$17.5 billion** from banks, fresh off a bond sale, to fuel its ongoing AI investments. This substantial debt accumulation underscores the exorbitant sums companies are committing to keep pace in the AI arms race. According to the **Ramp AI Index**, "AI-pilled" firms—those deeply committed to AI adoption—are spending an average of **$7,500 per employee each month** on AI solutions.

## WHAT TO WATCH
As AI giants go public, anticipate increased transparency demands and heightened scrutiny from investors and regulators alike regarding AI safety, ethical development, and market dominance. The continued push for "agentic" AI will lead to more autonomous tools, raising questions about human oversight and the future of work, especially as reports emerge of workers spending an average of **6.4 hours per week** "botsitting" AI.
